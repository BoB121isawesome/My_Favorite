Avatar: The Last Airbender is an American animated television series created by Michael Dante DiMartino and Bryan Konietzko for Nickelodeon. It aired for three seasons, from February 21, 2005, to July 19, 2008, on Nickelodeon's Nicktoons block. The series is set in an Asiatic-inspired world in which some people can telekinetically manipulate one of the four elements—water, earth, fire or air—through practices known as "bending", inspired by Chinese martial arts. The story follows the adventures of Aang, a young airbender who is the Avatar, the only person who can control all four elements. Aang is frozen in an iceberg for 100 years, and when he awakens, he finds that the Fire Nation has conquered most of the world. Aang must now master the other three elements and stop the Fire Nation from destroying the world. Aang is accompanied on his journey by Katara, a waterbender from the Southern Water Tribe, and Sokka, her brother. The trio travel to the Earth Kingdom, where they meet Toph Beifong, a blind earthbender who becomes Aang's earthbending teacher. The group also meets Zuko, a firebender prince who is banished from the Fire Nation by his father, Fire Lord Ozai. Zuko joins Aang's group on their journey in order to capture Aang and restore his honor. Along the way, Aang and his friends must face many challenges, including the Fire Nation, the Dai Li, a secret police force from the Earth Kingdom, and the spirit world. Aang must also learn to control his emotions and his powers, as he is the Avatar, the bridge between the human and spirit worlds. Avatar: The Last Airbender is a critically acclaimed series that has been praised for its complex characters, well-developed story, and beautiful animation. The series has won numerous awards, including two Primetime Emmy Awards and a Peabody Award. It has also been nominated for a Hugo Award and a BAFTA Award. From Wikipedia
